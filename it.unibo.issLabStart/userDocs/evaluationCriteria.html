Lab ISS | evaluationCriteria of pre-coding documents

Introduction

We report here the main criteria to be used for the (auto)evaluation of the deliverables related to the pre-coding phases (requirement analysis, problem analysis and project).

About content (there is no code without a project, no project without problem analysis and no problem without requirements)

The most important aspects of the document are:
  1. Quality of the logical architecture: the logical architecture (i.e. the result of requirement and problem analysis) is well described, well motivated and should be 'shared' by a wide set of different (working separately) analysts.
  2. Quality of the test plans: the test plans are closely related to the requirements; they clarify without ambiguity what a testing tool must find 'true' after the execution of the application.
  3. Quality of the project architecture: the logical architecture is well described, well related to the logical architecture and well motivated, by highlighting the choices (and their reason) made by the designer.

About substance

  1. Level of significance: the document is able to deliver useful information about the product and the process.
    The document should make a human reader (e.g. a project manager) able to:
    • understand what the customer 'really' needs
    • understand the main problems related to the product to be built
    • eveluate the effort (in time, resources, etc.) required to meet the requirements
    • organize the requirements into a ordered list of relevance and/or difficulty
    The document should make software developers able to:
    • acquire in a 'formal way' relevant aspects about the product, so to avoid the ambiguites of natural languages
    • fully understand the TestPlans, so to write in a short time a set of (acceptance) tests, before starting the coding
    • start the implementation of (a prototype) of the system, by implementing what is described in the project section

About form

We must evaluate also:
  1. The degree of logical consistence: the document does include no logical contradiction among its parts
  2. The degree of coherence: the document presents a good integration of diverse elements, relationships, or values
  3. The degree of synthesis: the document does not include useless verbosity
  4. The degree of relevance: the document does not include useless parts
  5. The degree of precision: the document attempts to express relevant aspects in a formal way, rather than using natural language
  6. The degree of dissemination: the document is written in English

About evaluation

The result of the evaluation should be expressed with reference to a scale ranging from A to F. Note that:
  1. An empty document (like templateToFill.html) sent with correct name (cognome_nome_xxx.pdf), and including correct author-data (with the photo) is qualified as F. Otherwise it cannot be classified.
  2. A document qualified with A presents no relevant part to be significatively improved
  3. A document qualified as B or C presents some part (not crtical) that can be improved
  4. A document qualified from D to F presents some relevant part that should be improved
After a (auto)evalutation of a document classified from B to F , it is important to higthligth the parts that require some improvement.
